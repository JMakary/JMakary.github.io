Bio

I'm currently a PhD student in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Dalhousie University. I'm supervised by Neil J. Ross, and I also work closely with Peter Selinger. My research interests are in Quantum Computation, and more specifically Categorical Quantum Mechanics.
Previously, I earned a Master's in Mathematics from Dalhousie University, where I was supervised by Neil J. Ross. My research focused on the Real Stabilizer Operators, and finding a finite presentation for them by way of generators and relations as a strict spatial monoidal groupoid. Before Dalhousie, I earned a BSc Double Hons in Mathematics and Computing Science from Saint Mary's University. I engaged in undergraduate research under Wendy Finbow-Singh in Rigidity Theory. I proved some basic results around rigidity involving planar methods, and developed Java algorithms to decide whether large structures were rigid
I have also been heavily involved in teaching duties since my undergraduate degree. I am a passionate teacher who hopes to continue to give students a fun, strong learning environment. Teaching Experience
